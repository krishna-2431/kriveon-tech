Product Requirements Document (PRD): KriveonTech Official Website
Document Owner: Senior Product Manager
Target Audience: Product Designers, UI/UX, Frontend & Backend Engineers, Project Managers
Status: Production-Ready
1. Executive Summary
KriveonTech requires a premium, high-converting digital storefront to transition its brand perception from an academic project center to a professional software engineering agency. The website will serve as an authoritative portfolio and lead-generation engine. Distinctively, this platform will eschew traditional lead-capture forms in favor of zero-friction, direct-communication channels (Phone and WhatsApp) to maximize conversion rates and immediate client engagement.
2. Product Vision
To be the most trusted and technically revered engineering partner for students, researchers, and startups, bridging the gap between theoretical academic research and production-grade software/hardware solutions.
3. Product Mission
Deliver a digital experience that mirrors the precision, quality, and modern architecture of the code we write, educating our audience on our rigorous engineering standards while providing frictionless avenues for project consultation.
4. Business Objectives
Brand Elevation: Position KriveonTech alongside modern, premium tech brands (e.g., Stripe, Vercel).
Trust Generation: Clearly communicate the 8-step project lifecycle and standard deliverables (GitHub repo, architecture diagrams, KT sessions).
Frictionless Lead Generation: Drive inbound inquiries exclusively through direct Phone and WhatsApp channels.

6. User Personas
Persona 1: The Stressed Academic (B.Tech/M.Tech Student)
Needs: Implementation of a complex IEEE paper, clean source code, and a comprehensive Knowledge Transfer (KT) session to survive their Viva.
Behavior: Skims for specific tech stacks (e.g., Python, IoT) and looks for proof of reliability.
Persona 2: The Innovator (Researcher/Startup Founder)
Needs: A functional prototype or MVP based on proprietary research.
Behavior: Evaluates the agency's professional standards, architecture diagrams, and testing methodologies.
7. User Journey
Discovery: User lands on the homepage via SEO or direct link.
Validation: User sees the premium dark-mode UI, reads the value proposition, and feels reassured by the professional aesthetic.
Education: User scrolls to the "Development Process" and "Deliverables" sections, understanding exactly what they get (Code, GitHub, KT, Docs).
Conversion: User clicks the sticky "Chat on WhatsApp" or "Call Now" button.
Resolution: User is instantly connected to a KriveonTech engineer.
8. Pain Points (Addressed by the Product)
Competitor Sites look cheap: Users fear getting scammed or receiving plagiarized code. Solution: Premium UI and transparent workflow.
Forms are tedious: Users don't want to wait 48 hours for a quote. Solution: Direct WhatsApp/Phone routing.
Lack of understanding: Users fear they won't understand the code they buy. Solution: Highlighting the dedicated Knowledge Transfer (KT) deliverable.
9. User Stories
As a visitor, I want to see a clear list of deliverables so I know exactly what I am paying for.
As a visitor, I want to contact the team immediately without filling out a form, so I can get my questions answered fast.
As a mobile user, I want the WhatsApp button to open my app directly with a pre-filled greeting.
As an engineering student, I want to see the specific hardware and software technologies KriveonTech supports.
10. Functional Requirements
Dynamic CTA Routing: "Chat on WhatsApp" buttons must utilize the wa.me API with pre-populated message templates based on the page context.
Sticky Contact Bar: A persistent header or floating action button (FAB) for instant contact on mobile devices.
No Form Inputs: The system architecture must not include any user-facing input forms (no POST requests for leads).
Responsive Asset Loading: SVGs and optimized WebP images must load conditionally based on viewport size.
11. Non-Functional Requirements
Hosting & Deployment: Deployed on edge networks (e.g., Vercel or Cloudflare Pages) for global low-latency access.
Security: Enforced HTTPS, strict Content Security Policy (CSP), and HSTS.
Maintainability: Built with a modern component-based framework (e.g., Next.js, React) to allow easy content updates.
12. Information Architecture
A flat, highly accessible hierarchy designed to prevent users from getting lost. All critical paths lead to the Contact actions.
13. Navigation Structure
Header (Sticky): Logo (Left) | Home, Services, Process, FAQ (Center) | Call Now, WhatsApp (Right - Highlighted)
Footer: Logo, Tagline | Quick Links | Legal (Privacy, Terms) | Contact Info (Phone numbers).

17. Content Strategy
Tone: Objective, expert, reassuring, and precise.
Vocabulary: Use proper engineering terminology (e.g., "Source Code," "Architecture Diagram," "Knowledge Transfer," "IoT") to build immediate credibility with technical audiences.
Exclusions: Explicitly state what is not included (e.g., "Project Report is NOT included") to manage expectations perfectly.
18. SEO Requirements
Metadata: Unique <title> and <meta name="description"> for every page targeting keywords like "IEEE paper implementation," "custom software engineering," "IoT project development."
Schema Markup: Implement LocalBusiness and Service JSON-LD schema.
Semantic HTML: Strict use of <h1>, <h2>, <article>, <section> tags.
19. Accessibility Requirements (WCAG 2.1 AA)
Contrast: Ensure the Electric Blue on Dark Background passes contrast ratios for text.
Keyboard Navigation: All CTAs and links must be fully focusable with visible focus rings.
Aria Labels: Required on all icon-only buttons (like the WhatsApp FAB).
20. Performance Requirements
Asset Optimization: Next/Image or equivalent for automatic webp conversion and lazy loading.
Font Loading: Preload primary fonts to prevent FOIT (Flash of Invisible Text).
JS Bundling: Code-splitting to ensure the initial payload is under 100kb.
21. Responsive Design Requirements
Breakpoints: Mobile (<768px), Tablet (768px - 1024px), Desktop (>1024px).
Mobile Specifics: The navigation must collapse into a clean hamburger menu. The WhatsApp FAB must be prominent and easily tappable with a thumb.
22. UX Principles
Zero Friction: The user should never have to think about how to contact the company.
Show, Don't Tell: Use architecture diagrams and component trees to illustrate "Clean Code" rather than just writing the words.
Predictability: The layout should follow established SaaS patterns (e.g., Stripe, Linear) so the user intuitively knows where to find information.
23. UI Guidelines & Design Language
Theme: Modern Dark Mode (Backgrounds in shades of #0A0A0A to #111111).
Primary Color: Electric Blue (#007AFF or similar vibrant tech blue).
Accent: Soft Gradient Blue (mixing primary blue with a deep cyan for hover states).
Typography: A premium geometric sans-serif (e.g., Inter, Geist) for UI elements. A monospace font (e.g., JetBrains Mono) for code snippets or technical accents.
Visual Style: Glassmorphism (subtle translucent backgrounds with background-blur), 1px subtle borders on cards, generous padding (padding: 2rem standard), rounded UI (border-radius: 8px to 12px).
24. CTA Strategy
Primary Action: "Call Now" (tel:+917670865257 / tel:+919014012864).
Secondary Action: "Chat on WhatsApp" (Links to WhatsApp API).
Placement: Global header, floating action button, end of every service page, bottom of the homepage.
25. Trust Building Strategy
Explicitly list the 12 Deliverables (Complete Source Code, DB Files, API Docs). This radically differentiates KriveonTech from competitors who hide what the client actually receives.
Prominently feature the "Knowledge Transfer Session" and "Support Until Viva" to alleviate post-delivery anxiety.
26. Competitive Positioning
Unlike traditional project centers that treat projects as commodities, KriveonTech operates as a boutique software agency. The brand signals high-end engineering, transparent workflows, and uncompromising quality. We do not compete on being the "cheapest"; we compete on being the "most reliable and professional."
27. Risks
Conversion Tracking: Without form submissions, tracking exact ad conversions (e.g., Google Ads) relies on click-tracking on the Phone/WhatsApp buttons, which is less accurate than a "Thank You" page.
Spam: Open WhatsApp links can attract generic spam.
28. Assumptions
The target audience prefers direct messaging/calling over filling out asynchronous forms.
The dark-theme, premium aesthetic will resonate positively with engineering students and tech-forward clients.
29. Future Roadmap (Phase 2 & Potential Additions)
Once the core platform is established, the following modules can be evaluated for Phase 2:
Client Portal: A secure login area for clients to track their 8-step project lifecycle in real-time.
Admin Dashboard: Internal tool for KriveonTech to manage inquiries and update project statuses.
Portfolio / Case Studies: Detailed write-ups of successful non-NDA projects with architectural breakdowns.
Payment Gateway: Integration for processing initial milestone payments securely.
Note: Online Quotation Requests and lengthy forms remain off the roadmap to preserve the direct-contact strategy.
